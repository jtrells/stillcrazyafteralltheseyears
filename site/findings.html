Findings

What US States are the most different population-wise?

The United States age distribution trend presents a similar percentage of population between ages 0-50 and then starts decaying; faster for ages 70+. This trend is similar in states like Georgia, California, Illinois and others. Nevertheless, some states like Maine, New Hampshire or Vermont have different distributions where one age range between 40-50s is predominant, and younger and older populations are much smaller. District of Columbia has a similar behavior but its predominant age is between 20-30s. However, these latest populations are much smaller than bigger areas like California, Illinois or Georgia.

Utah has an interesting distribution where there are more younger people being the kids predominant. From there, the percentages decay in a smoother way.

Aging

Japan and Germany are the two populations of the dataset with more aging people. At 2014, about 1/3 of Japan's population (~40M) was over 60 years old. Several news articles discuss this topic. Regarding Germany, about 27% of its population (~21.5M) is over 60.


However, Peru and Mexico have a bigger population of young people which could be caused by several reasons. One of them is the lack of sexual education among younger people that leads to a failure in birth control methods. Moreover, Peru and Mexico besides being latino countries, are places were the Catholic Church still have a big influence on decisions like sexual education on schools, use of birth control methods, abortion, and so on.
