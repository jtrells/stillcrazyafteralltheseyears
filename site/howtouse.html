How to Use?

The application allows you to select at most 4 different places (Country or US state) to display a pair of charts per selection. The charts, a pie chart and a bar chart, show the age distribution of the selection in a specific age range (per year or per 10 years). After selecting the places, you may select from a list of events up to 10 of them and see in the charts, how much people may remember those events (If at that time the person was at least 12 years old, he may remember it).

If needed, you can replace a place selection. Just click again on the place to make it go away!

Access application

From the menu bar at the top of the screen, select the Application option.

Selection controls

The left side of the application show the places and events selection options. For convenience, at the left most part of the screen, the user can select to add data from the whole United States or individual states. Next to these controls, a experimental map may be used to select a state based on its geographic location.

To select another country, use the toogle button bar below the map. The available options include Per√∫, Uruguay, Japan, Germany and Mexico. To deselect any option, just click on it again.

On the lower right zone of the selection controls area, you can find a list of interesting Events in Soccer History. Each line displays a different event: the event name on the left side and its year on the right. Just click on any event to see how the charts are updated.

Pie Chart

The bar chart shows slices in proportion to the population amount given an age range. Thus, slices with bigger areas have more population. Each slice color is randomly selected. At the center of the chart, you can see the name of the place selected and its total population in a given year. Around the chart, the slices' labels are displayed. However, if there are too many slices the labels will be hidden. To see further detail of a slice just place the mouse over it and a tooltip will appear.

When an event is selected, the chart will add one concentric circumference. The black portion represents the amount of the population that may remember the event. Thus, the grey portion represents the people that were not at least 12 years old when the event happened. To get more information about the event, place the mouse over the area of interest. The chart supports up to 10 events.

Bar Chart

Likewise the pie chart, a bar chart shows the distribution of age population for the selected place. The X-Axis represents the age ranges while the Y-Axis the population number (in thousands). Therefore, each bar represents the amount of people for a given place in a certain year. Although this chart does not show the name or year of the selection, you can get that information from the pie chart as they both work as a pair (and the pie chart will always be over the bar chart).

When an event is selected, the chart will add one horizontal bar below the graphic. The bar represents the amount of people that may remember the event. If the age range is not per year, the bar's start point will try to approximate the portion of the X-Axis range that applies for the event. Moreover, the event name and percentage are shown but you can place the mouse over the bar to see further information.

Charts controls

Two types of controls are places below the charts area. The first one allows you to change the Age Change. The second allows to change the Year of each chart. Even though some data had information for 2015, most of it only had data up to 2014. Therefore, the application only shows data up to 2014.
